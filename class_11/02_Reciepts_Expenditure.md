# 💰 1. What are Receipts?

**Receipts = Money received by the business**

👉 Example (Shopkeeper Ravi):

* Customer pays ₹5,000 → Receipt
* Bank gives loan ₹20,000 → Receipt

👉 So, every inflow of money = Receipt ✔️

---

# 📥 2. Types of Receipts

## (A) Revenue Receipts (Recurring)

These are **regular/normal business incomes**.

👉 Features:

* Happens again and again
* Related to daily business

👉 Examples:

* Sale of goods
* Commission received
* Interest received

👉 Example:
Ravi sells goods daily → this is **revenue receipt**

---

## (B) Capital Receipts (Non-Recurring)

These are **not regular** and affect business structure.

👉 Features:

* One-time or rare
* Not from daily operations

👉 Examples:

* Loan from bank
* Owner brings capital
* Sale of machinery

👉 Example:
Ravi takes ₹50,000 loan → **capital receipt**

---

# 💸 3. What is Expenditure?

**Expenditure = Money spent by business (payment)**

👉 Example:

* Paying rent
* Buying goods
* Paying salary

---

# 📤 4. Types of Expenditure

## (A) Revenue Expenditure (Recurring)

Spent for **day-to-day operations**

👉 Features:

* Short-term benefit
* Repeated regularly

👉 Examples:

* Rent
* Salary
* Electricity bill

👉 Example:
Ravi pays ₹2,000 rent every month → revenue expenditure

---

## (B) Capital Expenditure (Non-Recurring)

Spent to **buy long-term assets**

👉 Features:

* Long-term benefit
* Not frequent

👉 Examples:

* Buying machinery
* Buying furniture
* Buying building

👉 Example:
Ravi buys furniture ₹10,000 → capital expenditure

---

## (C) Deferred Revenue Expenditure

**Big expense now, but benefit for many years**

👉 Features:

* Not fully used in one year
* Spread over multiple years

👉 Examples:

* Heavy advertisement
* Big promotional campaign

👉 Example:
Ravi spends ₹50,000 on advertisement → benefit comes for years

---

# ⚠️ 5. Expense vs Expenditure (Very Important)

### 🔹 Expenditure

* Total money spent (can be capital or revenue)

### 🔹 Expense

* Only **revenue part used/consumed**

---

### 👉 Example:

Ravi buys machinery ₹1,00,000

* This is **Expenditure (capital)**
* But yearly depreciation ₹10,000 = **Expense**

---

### 👉 Simple Difference:

| Basis   | Expenditure        | Expense            |
| ------- | ------------------ | ------------------ |
| Meaning | Total spending     | Used/consumed part |
| Nature  | Capital or Revenue | Only Revenue       |
| Example | Buying machine     | Rent, salary       |

---

# 📊 6. What is Income?

**Income = Revenue − Expenses**

👉 Example:

* Sales = ₹50,000
* Expenses = ₹30,000

👉 Income = ₹20,000

---

### ⚠️ Important:

👉 Income and Profit are often similar, but:

* **Income** = from operations
* **Profit** = overall result

---

# 📈 7. What is Profit?

**Profit = Total Revenue − Total Expenses**

👉 Example:

* Total Revenue = ₹1,00,000
* Total Expenses = ₹70,000

👉 Profit = ₹30,000

---

# 📉 8. What is Loss?

**Loss = Expenses > Revenue**

👉 Example:

* Revenue = ₹50,000
* Expenses = ₹60,000

👉 Loss = ₹10,000

---

# 💎 9. What is Gain?

**Gain = Extra benefit not from normal business**

👉 Examples:

* Selling old machine at profit
* Winning a case in court

👉 Example:
Machine value = ₹20,000
Sold for ₹25,000
👉 Gain = ₹5,000

---

# 🎯 Quick Summary

* Receipts = Money received
* Expenditure = Money spent
* Revenue = Regular
* Capital = One-time
* Expense = Used part of expenditure
* Profit = Revenue − Expense
* Loss = Opposite of profit
* Gain = Extra/rare benefit
