# ✅ PART 1: Assets or Liabilities

### Q1 Answers:

1. Cash → **Asset**
2. Bank Loan → **Liability**
3. Furniture → **Asset**
4. Creditors → **Liability**
5. Stock → **Asset**

👉 Assets = what business owns
👉 Liabilities = what business owes

---

# ✅ PART 2: Debtor or Creditor

### Q2 Answers:

1. Goods sold on credit to Ramesh → **Debtor**
2. Goods purchased on credit from Suresh → **Creditor**

👉 Debtor = gives money to business
👉 Creditor = takes money from business

---

# ✅ PART 3: Capital vs Revenue Receipts

### Q3 Answers:

1. Sale of goods → **Revenue Receipt**
2. Loan from bank → **Capital Receipt**
3. Interest received → **Revenue Receipt**
4. Owner brings cash → **Capital Receipt**

---

# ✅ PART 4: Debit and Credit

### Q4 Answers:

1. Started business with ₹50,000

   * Cash A/c → **Debit**
   * Capital A/c → **Credit**

2. Purchased furniture ₹10,000

   * Furniture A/c → **Debit**
   * Cash A/c → **Credit**

3. Sold goods ₹5,000

   * Cash A/c → **Debit**
   * Sales A/c → **Credit**

4. Paid rent ₹2,000

   * Rent A/c → **Debit**
   * Cash A/c → **Credit**

👉 Trick:

* What comes in → Debit
* What goes out → Credit

---

# ✅ PART 5: Short Answers

### Q5. Business Entity:

Business is treated as separate from owner.
👉 Example: Ravi’s shop is different from Ravi personally.

---

### Q6. Transaction:

Any activity involving money in business.
👉 Examples:

* Buying goods
* Paying rent

---

### Q7 Definitions:

* **Asset** → Anything owned by business
* **Liability** → Amount to be paid by business

# ✅ PART 7: Capital Calculation

### Q10 Answers:

1. Capital = 80,000 − 30,000 = **₹50,000**
2. Capital = 1,50,000 − 70,000 = **₹80,000**

---

# ✅ PART 8: Real-Life Case

### Q11 Solution:

Ravi’s Shop:

**Step 1: Assets**

* Cash = ₹1,00,000
* Goods = ₹20,000
* Furniture = ₹10,000

👉 Total Assets = ₹1,30,000

---

**Step 2: Liabilities**

* Loan = ₹30,000

---

**Step 3: Capital**
Capital = Assets − Liabilities
= 1,30,000 − 30,000
= **₹1,00,000**
