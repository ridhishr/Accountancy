Here’s a **simple and clear explanation** of all topics with examples 👇

---

## **1. Meaning of Accounting**

**Accounting** means **recording, classifying, summarizing and analyzing financial transactions** of a business.

👉 In simple words:
It is the process of keeping track of money coming in and going out.

**Example:**
If a shopkeeper buys goods for ₹10,000 and sells for ₹15,000, accounting records both purchase and sale and shows profit ₹5,000.

---

## **2. Meaning of Accountancy**

**Accountancy** is a **broader concept**. It includes **principles, rules and knowledge of accounting**.

👉 In simple words:
Accountancy is the **theory (study)**, while accounting is the **practical work**.

**Example:**
Learning rules like “Debit what comes in, Credit what goes out” is **accountancy**, applying it in books is **accounting**.

---

## **3. Characteristics of Accountancy**

1. **Systematic Process**

   * Work is done step-by-step (record → classify → summarize)
   * Example: First journal entry, then ledger, then final accounts.

2. **Based on Rules and Principles**

   * Follows standard rules (like accounting standards).
   * Example: Depreciation is calculated using proper methods.

3. **Records Financial Transactions Only**

   * Only money-related transactions are recorded.
   * Example: Owner’s skill is not recorded, but salary paid is recorded.

4. **Helps in Decision Making**

   * Provides useful information to owners.
   * Example: Profit helps decide whether to expand business.

5. **Provides True and Fair View**

   * Shows correct financial position.
   * Example: Balance sheet shows assets and liabilities clearly.

---

## **4. Objectives of Accounting**

1. **To Maintain Records**

   * Keep proper records of all transactions.
   * Example: Daily sales recorded in books.

2. **To Calculate Profit or Loss**

   * Know whether business is earning or losing.
   * Example: Profit = Sales – Expenses.

3. **To Show Financial Position**

   * Shows assets and liabilities.
   * Example: Balance sheet shows cash, stock, loans.

4. **To Help in Decision Making**

   * Helps owner take business decisions.
   * Example: Whether to invest more or not.

5. **To Provide Information to Users**

   * Information for investors, banks, government.
   * Example: Bank checks accounts before giving loan.

---

## **5. Difference Between Bookkeeping and Accounting**

| Basis          | Bookkeeping              | Accounting                      |
| -------------- | ------------------------ | ------------------------------- |
| Meaning        | Recording transactions   | Analyzing and interpreting data |
| Scope          | Limited                  | Wide                            |
| Stage          | First step               | Second step                     |
| Skill required | Basic                    | Higher knowledge                |
| Example        | Writing sales in journal | Finding profit from data        |

👉 **Simple Example:**

* Bookkeeping = Writing “Sold goods ₹5,000”
* Accounting = Calculating total profit from all sales and expenses

---

## **6. Difference Between Accounting and Accountancy**

| Basis   | Accounting                               | Accountancy                   |
| ------- | ---------------------------------------- | ----------------------------- |
| Meaning | Practical work of recording transactions | Study of principles and rules |
| Nature  | Practical                                | Theoretical                   |
| Scope   | Narrow                                   | Wide                          |
| Focus   | Recording & reporting                    | Knowledge & framework         |
| Example | Preparing balance sheet                  | Learning how to prepare it    |

👉 **Simple Example:**

* Accounting = Making accounts in Tally or books
* Accountancy = Studying how accounts should be made

---

## ✅ **Final Simple Summary**

* **Accounting** = Doing the work (recording money transactions)
* **Accountancy** = Studying the rules of accounting
* **Bookkeeping** = Writing transactions
* **Accounting (next step)** = Understanding and analyzing them
