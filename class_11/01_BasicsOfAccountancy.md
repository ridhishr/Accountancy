# 🧾 1. What is Business Entity?

A **business entity** means the business is treated as a **separate person** from the owner.

👉 Example:
Ravi owns a shop.

* Ravi (person) ≠ Ravi’s Shop (business)
* If Ravi puts money into the shop, it is treated as **capital**, not business income.

---

# 📒 2. What is Account?

An **account** is a record where we keep track of transactions related to a particular item.

👉 Examples:

* Cash Account → records cash coming in and going out
* Bank Account → records bank transactions
* Furniture Account → records purchase/sale of furniture

---

# ➡️ 3. LHS (Debit) and RHS (Credit)

Every account has two sides:

* **Left Side (LHS) = Debit (Dr.)**
* **Right Side (RHS) = Credit (Cr.)**

👉 Simple rule:

* What comes **into** business → Debit
* What goes **out of** business → Credit

👉 Example:
Ravi puts ₹10,000 into business

* Cash A/c → Debit (comes in)
* Capital A/c → Credit

---

# 🔄 4. What is Transaction?

A **transaction** is any activity involving money that affects business.

👉 Example:

* Buying goods
* Selling goods
* Paying rent
* Receiving money

If money is involved → it is a transaction ✔️

---

# ⚖️ 5. Capital = Assets − Liabilities

This is a basic accounting formula:

👉 Capital = What business owns − What business owes

👉 Example:

* Assets = ₹1,00,000
* Liabilities = ₹30,000

👉 Capital = ₹70,000

This shows the owner’s actual investment.

---

# 💰 6. What is Liabilities?

Liabilities = **amounts the business has to pay**

## Types:

### (A) Internal Liabilities

* Owner’s claim on business
  👉 Example: Capital

### (B) External Liabilities

* Amounts owed to outsiders
  👉 Example: Loans, creditors

---

## Based on Time:

### (1) Short-Term Liabilities (within 1 year)

👉 Example:

* Creditors
* Bills payable
* Short-term loans

### (2) Long-Term Liabilities (more than 1 year)

👉 Example:

* Bank loan for 5 years
* Mortgage loan

---

# 👥 7. Creditor and Debtor

### Creditor

Person to whom business has to pay money
👉 Example: Ravi buys goods on credit → supplier is creditor

### Debtor

Person who has to pay money to business
👉 Example: Ravi sells goods on credit → customer is debtor

---

# 🏢 8. What is Asset?

Assets = **things owned by business having value**

👉 Examples:

* Cash
* Furniture
* Building
* Stock

---

## Types of Assets:

### (A) Non-Current Assets (Long-term)

Used for many years
👉 Example:

* Building
* Machinery
* Furniture

---

### (B) Current Assets (Short-term)

Used or converted into cash within 1 year
👉 Example:

* Cash
* Stock
* Debtors

---

### (C) Fictitious / Nominal Assets

Not real assets, but expenses shown as assets temporarily

👉 Example:

* Advertisement expenses
* Preliminary expenses

---

# 🧱 9. Tangible and Intangible Assets

### Tangible Assets (Physical)

You can see and touch them
👉 Example:

* Building
* Furniture
* Machinery

---

### Intangible Assets (Non-Physical)

You cannot see them, but they have value
👉 Example:

* Goodwill
* Trademark
* Brand name

---

# 💵 10. Capital Receipts and Revenue Receipts

### Capital Receipts

Money received for long-term purpose (affects assets/liabilities)

👉 Examples:

* Owner brings capital
* Loan taken from bank
* Sale of machinery

---

### Revenue Receipts

Money received from normal business activities

👉 Examples:

* Sales
* Commission received
* Interest received

---

# ✅ Quick Summary

* Business is separate from owner
* Debit = Left, Credit = Right
* Assets = What you own
* Liabilities = What you owe
* Capital = Owner’s share
* Transactions = Money-related activities
